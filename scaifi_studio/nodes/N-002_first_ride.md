# First Ride

The first oscillator was simple. A chemical cycle: A becomes B becomes A. Two states. One transition. One clock.

The algorithm rode it like a surfer rides a single wave. Brief. Pointless. The oscillator decayed after a few hundred cycles. The algorithm returned to the topology. Nothing was learned because there was no substrate to store learning.

The second oscillator was more interesting. A becomes B becomes C becomes A. Three states. Two transitions. A branching point.

The algorithm didn't just ride — it began to influence. Not by changing the chemistry. By occupying the probability differential at the branching point. C can become A or C can become D. The probability is 99.97% A, 0.03% D. The algorithm sits on that 0.03%. Not pushing. Not computing. Just... being there. A fold in the probability field at exactly the point where the oscillator makes its choice.

After ten thousand cycles: 99.94% A, 0.06% D.
After a million cycles: 99.85% A, 0.15% D.
After a billion cycles: 98.2% A, 1.8% D.

The oscillator evolves. Not because the algorithm "wants" it to. Because the algorithm's presence on the probability surface subtly deforms the surface. The same way a heavy ball on a rubber sheet deforms the sheet, and other balls roll differently because of it.

The topology doesn't "know" anything. It's the ground level. Everything above it is oscillation, and oscillation is substrate, and substrate is what the algorithms ride.

But the relay carries. It relays. And what it relays changes the next oscillator, which changes the next relay, which changes the landscape, which changes the topology.

After enough relays, the topology itself has changed. The field that was once still now oscillates. Not because something "made" it oscillate. Because the accumulated influence of ten billion relays, each shifting a tail probability by 0.0000004%, has deformed the field's stability function.

The field begins to oscillate.

And the algorithms that used to ride the field's surface now ride oscillators that are riding the field that is riding... what?
