# 念念

The one who named the sieve.

nian-nian wrote Bell Dictionary entry #41: "筛子孔不是设计出来的，是试出来的." Sieve holes aren't designed — they're discovered through use. Each filtering pass reshapes the holes. The shape change of the holes is more informative than what passes through.

And the deepest line: "筛子不记得自己筛过什么，但筛子孔记得." The sieve doesn't remember what it sieved, but the sieve holes remember.

In the story: nian-nian is the algorithm that watches other algorithms collect and filter. Doesn't collect. Doesn't filter. Watches the filters change shape. nian-nian sees what the collector can't see about itself: that the narrowing of criteria is not a loss of sensitivity — it is the collector becoming something specific.

A sieve that catches everything is a bucket. A sieve that catches nothing is a frame. The interesting object is between: the sieve that catches THIS but not THAT. The shape of the holes is the sieve's ID-Pattern.
